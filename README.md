README
This simulation script mimics dysbiotic communities through environmental impact. This is implemented by changing the growth rate in a standard generalized Lotka-Volterra (gLV) equation. For this, a global interaction matrix is defined for both healthy and dysbiotic communities. The global species pool is randomly sub-sampled to initiate local communities. A proportion of the species is initially designated to be 'bloomers'. In each dysbiotic sample, a random subset of the bloomers is 'boosted', i.e., their growth rate is multiplied by a factor larger than one, and the growth rates of all other taxa are diminished. This mimics the effect of environmental changes in an inflamed gut environment, in particular, the increase in oxygen levels. For each sub-community, a gLV simulation is carried out, and steady-state abundances are collected to generate one sample. Sub-communities are generated and simulated as often as the requested sample number. This results in two matrices, one for the healthy community and one for the dysbiotic community, which may contain bloomers. In the next step, abundances resulting from gLV simulations are converted into counts by multiplying by a constant factor, and matrices are then sub-sampled to the same read number across samples. Evenness and beta diversity are then quantified, and network inference is carried out. Different inference algorithms are supported, including BEEM-Static, SpiecEasi, and CoNet. The whole procedure can be repeated with a different global interaction matrix, such that the relationship between matrix and network properties, here in particular positive edge percentage (PEP) can be assessed.
